1

Equity in QI ⓘ QI systematically measures and improves healthcare by analyzing outcome disparities across demographics.

KHS’s Quality Improvement Program now systematically stratifies health outcomes by demographics to identify disparities—reviewing measures like cancer screening and diabetes control across groups and regions. Targeted interventions close identified gaps.

Result: Equity is built into success metrics, with dashboards spotlighting where to focus improvement.

2

Health Equity Grants ⓘ A $20M grant program funding providers and strategic initiatives to expand care in underserved communities.

In Aug 2023, KHS Board approved a $20M Health Equity Grants program: $8M for provider recruitment and quality projects, plus $12M for strategic initiatives supporting community organizations in underserved areas.

Result: More clinics and programs in remote Kern communities, funding innovations—e.g., expanding diabetic care outreach for farmworkers.

3

Doula & Maternal Health ⓘ Medi‑Cal covers doula services providing community-based prenatal and childbirth support.

Since Jan 1, 2023, Medi‑Cal doula services are a covered benefit. KHS integrates trained birth coaches into care teams and educates communities, boosting use among Black mothers and rural members.

Result: Increased prenatal support and real‑world outcomes—community sessions led to higher doula utilization.

4

Community Health Workers ⓘ Community members trained to guide peers through health services, building trust and cultural competency.

KHS employs CHWs with lived experience, hiring from the communities they serve to bridge cultural and linguistic gaps. They provide outreach, education, coordination, and resource connections.

Result: Trusted, culturally competent support helps members navigate care and social needs, reducing barriers one person at a time.

5

ECM & Supports ⓘ Enhanced Care Management plus social services (housing, food, sobering) for whole-person health.

Under CalAIM, KHS expanded Enhanced Care Management and Community Supports, adding 31 ECM programs and offering 12 of 14 state-approved social services (housing, food assistance, sobering centers).

Result: Whole‑person care reduces ER visits and tackles social determinants—stable housing and food security improve member outcomes.
